Instructions to use the simulator for individual photovoltaic production

Step 1 - Location:

We must identify the latitude and longitude coordinates where the installation will be carried out, for which there are several options:

a)    By scrolling on the map.

b)    Identifying an exact area or address in the <Location> field.

Once the exact place is displayed, double-click with the mouse to complete the Long-Lat field.

At this moment the application is ready to start the calculation requesting PVGIS to determine the optimal orientation and inclination.

If you know it, you can indicate a second point that informs the east-west orientation of the panels, for which you can use the satellite view.

Also, if you know it, you can enter the angle with respect to the ground in the <Inclination> field.

Once you have completed this information, you can click on the “Next” button.

Step 2 – Consumption data:

Select the fee type (2.0TD or 3.0TD) to be used in the simulation. Check prices. Those of SOM Energia are initially proposed. If they are not correct, they will be updated to the appropriate values before performing the calculation.

·       If you have a CSV file with hourly consumption obtained from your distribution company, select the option (Individual consumption file) and upload it using the <Choose file> button.

·       If you do not have it, use the REE Type Profile option and enter the annual consumption of the building.

You will see a colored map with your consumption profile.

Once you have reached this point, you can click on the “Next” button.

Step 3:


The results of the first proposal will be observed in the <Energy Balance>, <Economic Balance> and <Graphics> tabs, having used the economic values with the fees defined in step 2.

Step 4:

In the <Economic balance> tab, the data corresponding to the possible subvention that may correspond to the installation will be completed, taking into account that the EU subvention is limited to the fact that the annual production of the panels does not exceed 80% of the annual consumption.

Regarding the IBI, the value paid can be indicated and, depending on the municipality in question, what is the percentage that is subsidized and for how many years.

Step 5:

Several test cycles can be done by changing the installation configuration <Panels> or <Power per panel>. This change can be made from the <Energy Balance> tab or from the Alternatives graph in the <Economic Balance> tab. The application will perform the corresponding calculations automatically.

Step 6:

In the <Parameters> tab that is activated from the tools icon, several parameters used in the calculation can be updated. Perhaps the most important is the euro price per kWp installed. Changes made on this tab will be reflected in the next calculation.
